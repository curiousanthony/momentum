# Progression Stages

This document uses a standardized persona structure with the same fields for each
stage: `Summary`, `Goals`, `Behaviors`, `Frictions`, `Success Criteria`,
`Dashboard Needs`, and `Signals We Can Observe`.

The stages are not static identities. They describe the journey from first
adoption to high-leverage mastery with Cursor.

## Explorer

### Summary

A curious developer experimenting with Cursor and still forming a mental model of
what good AI-assisted development looks like.

### Goals

- understand what Cursor is good at
- get quick wins without losing confidence
- avoid feeling behind or incompetent

### Behaviors

- tries different prompts and workflows inconsistently
- experiments across small tasks
- relies on surface-level success signals

### Frictions

- unclear what counts as improvement
- hard to connect usage to real capability
- may bounce between excitement and skepticism

### Success Criteria

- feels early competence
- sees that small actions are producing real progress
- returns because the product feels useful, not gimmicky

### Dashboard Needs

- beginner-friendly explanations
- simple milestones
- visible early progress
- encouraging insight, not dense analytics

### Signals We Can Observe

- low total history
- inconsistent session cadence
- limited tool and model variety
- shallow but growing edit / prompt activity

## Growing Builder

### Summary

A developer actively leveling up with Cursor and wanting evidence that stronger
workflows and judgment are forming.

### Goals

- get better at collaborating with Cursor
- build confidence in AI-assisted development
- turn experimentation into repeatable capability

### Behaviors

- uses Cursor regularly across meaningful tasks
- starts noticing preferred models and workflows
- wants more than raw stats

### Frictions

- hard to know whether usage quality is improving
- can mistake activity for mastery
- may lose momentum after a rough session

### Success Criteria

- sees clear proof of capability growth
- identifies better working patterns
- feels on a path toward mastery

### Dashboard Needs

- interpretable skill signals
- progression stages and mastery framing
- momentum support
- insights tied to concrete behavior

### Signals We Can Observe

- rising session frequency
- more consistent tool usage
- broader project and language coverage
- increasing streak and achievement progress

## Momentum Builder

### Summary

A user with real competence who now needs consistency, rhythm, and recovery to
keep translating skill into sustained output.

### Goals

- keep a strong working cadence
- recover quickly from interruptions
- avoid falling back into erratic usage

### Behaviors

- has established preferred workflows
- cares about streaks and momentum
- measures weeks and months, not just sessions

### Frictions

- off days feel costly
- progress can feel invisible if only outcomes matter
- may struggle to recover after breaks

### Success Criteria

- keeps steady momentum
- sees recovery as part of growth
- feels supported rather than judged by the dashboard

### Dashboard Needs

- comeback loops
- weekly rhythm views
- momentum health signals
- reinforcement that values consistency over perfection

### Signals We Can Observe

- stable active-day patterns
- meaningful streak behavior
- repeated cross-session habits
- fewer long gaps between sessions

## Power User

### Summary

A developer who uses Cursor deeply and wants leverage, optimization, and
self-comparison against their own best patterns.

### Goals

- optimize workflows for speed and quality
- understand what differentiates great sessions
- increase leverage across projects

### Behaviors

- uses multiple tools and models intentionally
- works across larger or more complex codebases
- wants nuanced signals, not basic onboarding feedback

### Frictions

- shallow gamification feels insulting
- noisy metrics obscure real leverage
- the dashboard can become redundant if it stops teaching anything new

### Success Criteria

- learns non-obvious things about their own behavior
- gets high-signal comparative insights
- sees advanced mastery opportunities

### Dashboard Needs

- deeper pattern recognition
- advanced insights and trend views
- harder challenges
- richer project and model analysis

### Signals We Can Observe

- high lifetime usage
- strong model diversity or specialization
- higher subagent / tool depth
- richer project-level activity

## Strategic Builder

### Summary

A late-game user who employs Cursor intentionally to create outsized outcomes
across products, projects, or teams.

### Goals

- maximize leverage, not just activity
- connect AI-assisted behavior to shipped work
- make long-range decisions using reflected data

### Behaviors

- operates across multiple projects or initiatives
- thinks in systems, workflows, and outcomes
- wants a trusted command layer, not just motivational feedback

### Frictions

- local behavior data does not always cleanly map to business or product outcomes
- can outgrow simplistic progression systems
- needs context continuity across long horizons

### Success Criteria

- sees meaningful outcome-oriented signals
- can reason across projects and time horizons
- trusts the product as a strategic companion

### Dashboard Needs

- cross-project context
- stronger outcome framing
- durable memory and decision support
- high-signal summaries with minimal fluff

### Signals We Can Observe

- multi-project activity
- high aggregate XP and stage depth
- repeated advanced workflow patterns
- broader need for memory, roadmap, and history
