# User Jobs And Flows

This document uses a JTBD-oriented structure:

- `Job Story`
- `Trigger`
- `Push`
- `Pull`
- `Anxiety`
- `Habit`
- `Desired Outcome`
- `Key Signals`

## Job: Show Me That I'm Improving

### Job Story

When I have been using Cursor for a while, I want to understand whether I am
actually getting better, so I can feel confident that my effort is turning into
real capability.

### Trigger

The user has enough activity to suspect growth but cannot easily interpret it.

### Push

Memory is unreliable, and raw activity counts do not explain improvement.

### Pull

The dashboard can reveal interpretable progress, not just more numbers.

### Anxiety

What if I am just using Cursor more, not using it better?

### Habit

Developers often default to self-judgment based on vague memory rather than
evidence.

### Desired Outcome

The user leaves feeling clearer, more capable, and more motivated.

### Key Signals

- capability growth
- best-session patterns
- progress toward the next meaningful milestone

## Job: Help Me Regain Momentum

### Job Story

When my rhythm breaks or I have a rough session, I want the dashboard to help me
restart, so I can recover momentum without guilt.

### Trigger

The user loses streak, misses days, or feels discouraged after weak sessions.

### Push

Breaks make progress feel fragile and easy to lose.

### Pull

A good dashboard reframes recovery as a normal part of mastery.

### Anxiety

If I lost rhythm, maybe I am back to square one.

### Habit

Users often interpret inconsistency as failure instead of a recoverable dip.

### Desired Outcome

The user sees an easy comeback path and feels re-engaged quickly.

### Key Signals

- comeback opportunities
- clean, non-punitive momentum views
- lightweight next milestone prompts

## Job: What Kind Of Builder Am I Becoming?

### Job Story

When I want to understand my evolving identity with AI tools, I want to see what
stage I am in and what comes next, so I can feel that I am progressing toward
mastery.

### Trigger

The user starts caring about long-term growth, not just today's metrics.

### Push

Without a narrative arc, progress feels fragmented.

### Pull

Stage progression gives the user a coherent growth story.

### Anxiety

Am I still just experimenting, or am I becoming genuinely strong at this?

### Habit

People tend to focus on short-term wins and miss long-term identity change.

### Desired Outcome

The user sees a credible journey from early adoption to high-leverage use.

### Key Signals

- progression stage
- traits of the next stage
- mastery lanes or growth themes

## Job: What Should I Focus On Next?

### Job Story

When I understand my current state, I want a lightweight indication of what would
most help me level up next, so I can improve without overthinking.

### Trigger

The user sees interesting data but is unsure how to act on it.

### Push

Reflection without action can become passive dashboard browsing.

### Pull

The dashboard can suggest one or two high-leverage next moves.

### Anxiety

The product might become preachy, noisy, or over-prescriptive.

### Habit

Developers often postpone behavior change when the next step is vague.

### Desired Outcome

The user gets a small, credible next action grounded in observed behavior.

### Key Signals

- stage-specific recommendations
- opportunity gaps derived from usage patterns
- priority prompts with clear rationale
