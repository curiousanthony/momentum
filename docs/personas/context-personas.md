# Context Personas

These personas are secondary lenses layered on top of the primary progression
stages. They capture situational context rather than the main transformation arc.

The structure follows a standard persona format:

- `Context`
- `Goals`
- `Behaviors`
- `Success Criteria`
- `Risks`
- `Dashboard Needs`

## Ambitious Founder

### Context

Building products quickly, often juggling idea validation, feature shipping, and
business pressure at the same time.

### Goals

- move from idea to product faster
- stay motivated through uncertain product work
- feel that Cursor is increasing shipping leverage

### Behaviors

- switches between product thinking and implementation
- values speed but still needs trust and clarity
- works across multiple parallel initiatives

### Success Criteria

- feels momentum toward shipping
- sees which patterns help turn ideas into product progress
- uses the dashboard as a confidence amplifier, not a distraction

### Risks

- may reject features that feel playful but low-value
- can become impatient with analytics that do not map to outcomes

### Dashboard Needs

- outcome-oriented interpretation
- strong project memory
- concise guidance on where leverage is increasing

## Busy Professional

### Context

A high-performing engineer or tech lead with limited time and little tolerance
for noise.

### Goals

- get quick, trustworthy signal
- understand whether Cursor is improving effectiveness
- avoid spending time in a dashboard that feels ornamental

### Behaviors

- scans summaries rather than exploring every view
- values clarity, speed, and credibility
- prefers subtle motivation over overt gamification

### Success Criteria

- sees the most important changes in under a minute
- trusts the dashboard's interpretation
- keeps returning because it saves cognitive effort

### Risks

- may disengage if the product feels too game-like
- may ignore progression if it is not grounded in real value

### Dashboard Needs

- summary-first views
- premium tone
- highly interpretable insights
- minimal clutter

## Solo Power User

### Context

A developer who uses Cursor deeply every day and wants to squeeze more leverage
from already strong habits.

### Goals

- optimize workflow quality
- learn from long-term usage patterns
- turn high activity into strategic advantage

### Behaviors

- uses many features intentionally
- compares current performance to prior best performance
- cares about advanced workflows and nuanced signals

### Success Criteria

- finds non-obvious patterns and opportunities
- sees advanced progression or mastery states
- feels the product remains useful after basic motivation needs are solved

### Risks

- shallow badges, points, and streaks feel beneath the user
- generic advice creates immediate distrust

### Dashboard Needs

- deep insight
- advanced pattern views
- self-comparison over time
- stronger memory and decision support
