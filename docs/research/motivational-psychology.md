# Motivational Psychology

## Evidence

Three research themes are especially relevant to this product.

### Self-Determination Theory

Intrinsic motivation is strongest when people feel:

- `Autonomy`: I am acting by choice
- `Competence`: I am getting better
- `Relatedness`: my work matters and connects to something meaningful

### Goal-Gradient Effect

People increase effort when progress toward a meaningful goal becomes visible and
legible, especially near completion.

### Habit Formation And Streak Design

Streaks can reinforce consistency, but they can also create guilt, loss aversion,
and compulsive behavior when overemphasized.

### Reward Risks

Expected tangible rewards can reduce intrinsic motivation when they feel
controlling rather than informative.

## Interpretation

The dashboard should primarily support:

- competence through visible mastery
- autonomy through non-coercive guidance
- relatedness through meaningful framing around becoming a stronger builder

Streaks should exist, but as support for momentum rather than the emotional core
of the product.

## Product Implications

- use progress bars and milestones for meaningful next steps
- favor informative feedback over controlling rewards
- design comeback loops alongside streaks
- avoid pressure-heavy wording that turns reflection into obligation
