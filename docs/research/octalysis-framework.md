# Octalysis Framework

## Evidence

The Octalysis Framework organizes motivation into eight core drives and
distinguishes between more sustainable motivation and more manipulative pressure.

For `Cursor Dashboard`, the most relevant drives are:

- `CD2 Development and Accomplishment`
- `CD3 Empowerment of Creativity and Feedback`
- `CD1 Epic Meaning and Calling`
- `CD4 Ownership and Possession`

The more dangerous drives to overuse are:

- `CD6 Scarcity and Impatience`
- `CD8 Loss and Avoidance`

## Interpretation

This product should be built around mastery and empowerment, not fear of loss.
The user should feel they are becoming a stronger builder, not merely protecting
a streak or hoarding points.

## Product Mapping

### CD2 Development And Accomplishment

Use for:

- progression stages
- mastery lanes
- milestone unlocks
- visible capability growth

### CD3 Empowerment Of Creativity And Feedback

Use for:

- insight loops
- feedback on models, tools, and workflows
- richer interpretation of how the user works

### CD1 Epic Meaning And Calling

Use for:

- framing the product as a journey toward becoming an AI-native builder
- connecting progress to ambitious real-world building

### CD4 Ownership And Possession

Use for:

- persistent profile
- personal growth history
- project memory and handoff context

### CD5 Social Influence And Relatedness

Use cautiously for:

- social proof or examples
- optional, non-competitive comparison

### CD6 Scarcity And Impatience

Use sparingly for:

- sequencing unlocks
- staged access to advanced views

Avoid artificial FOMO.

### CD7 Unpredictability And Curiosity

Use for:

- insight reveals
- discovering hidden patterns

### CD8 Loss And Avoidance

Use very carefully for:

- light streak preservation awareness

Avoid building the product around fear of losing progress.

## Product Implications

- center the dashboard on mastery and creativity
- use curiosity to reveal insights, not manipulate compulsive checking
- keep loss-based mechanics secondary to recovery and growth
