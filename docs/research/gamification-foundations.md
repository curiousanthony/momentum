# Gamification Foundations

## Evidence

Research and practitioner frameworks converge on the idea that gamification works
best when it supports meaningful motivation, not when it relies only on points,
badges, and leaderboards.

Key takeaways:

- shallow gamification can increase short-term activity but often fails to create durable meaning
- points, levels, and leaderboards are not enough on their own
- developer audiences are especially likely to reject mechanics that feel manipulative or ornamental

## Interpretation

`Cursor Dashboard` should use deep gamification. The mechanics should reinforce a
real growth narrative:

- becoming better with Cursor
- sustaining momentum
- turning behavior into outcomes

That means the game layer must sit on top of genuine insight.

## Product Implications

- tie mechanics to meaningful behavior rather than vanity counts
- prioritize competence and insight over competition
- use self-comparison more than social comparison
- make progression interpretable, not merely collectible
